Characterizing Multimedia-rich OIE specific Tactics and Impact Assessment

Multimedia rich OIE specific tactics are multifaceted and use multiple modalities of information. We will explain how we characterize these tactics and conduct impact assessment. Various multimedia rich OIE specific tactics have been identified in our studies and from the literature. These tactics are characterized by their multi-faceted detection and impact assessment, i.e., network (N), content (C), and/or platform affordance (P). Each of these facets (network, content, and platform affordance) in turn requires analysis of a variety of information modalities (text, audio, video, images, network, URLs, metadata, algorithms, etc.). A content taxonomy of the various information modalities is included here

While some tactics leverage content, network, or platform affordances, others depend on a combination of two or all three facets. For instance, content cloning/repurposing leverages content facet, however, coordinated content push leverages both content and network facet. On the other hand, coordinated deep fake tactic leverages all three facets, i.e., network, content, and platform affordance.
